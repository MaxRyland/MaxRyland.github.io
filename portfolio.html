HW:8. Tell a Data story from the Talking Economics Festival.

Cash Withdrawals Data

My fifthteenth chart looks at cash withdrawals in in the UK between 2002 and 2018.

In his critique of cryptocurrency at the Talking Economics festival, John Turner states that digital money is not a new concept, arguing that we have been moving towards a cashless society for decades, citing that credit cards were introduced in the UK in 1966, debit cards in 1987 and contactless payments in 2007.

However, whilst these non-cash transaction methods have been around for decades and are growing in influence, society still has a strong reliance on cash transactions. Data shows that cash withdrawals have only recently been decreasing in the UK since its peak of 2.9 billion withdrawals in 2012.

It is difficult to assertively claim that we have been moving towards a cashless society for decades.
